## Description

You are given <code>nums</code>, an array of positive integers of size <code>2 \* n</code>. You must perform <code>n</code> operations on this array.

In the <code>i<sup>th</sup></code> operation (1-indexed), you will:

    - Choose two elements, <code>x</code> and <code>y</code>.
    - Receive a score of <code>i * gcd(x, y)</code>.
    - Remove <code>x</code> and <code>y</code> from <code>nums</code>.

Return _the maximum score you can receive after performing _<code>n</code>_ operations._

The function <code>gcd(x, y)</code> is the greatest common divisor of <code>x</code> and <code>y</code>.

Example 1:

<br />
<pre className="overflow-x-auto">
	Input: nums = [1,2] Output: 1 Explanation: The optimal choice of operations
	is: (1 * gcd(1, 2)) = 1
</pre>

Example 2:

<br />
<pre className="overflow-x-auto">
	Input: nums = [3,4,6,8] Output: 11 Explanation: The optimal choice of
	operations is: (1 * gcd(3, 6)) + (2 * gcd(4, 8)) = 3 + 8 = 11
</pre>

Example 3:

<br />
<pre className="overflow-x-auto">
	Input: nums = [1,2,3,4,5,6] Output: 14 Explanation: The optimal choice of
	operations is: (1 * gcd(1, 5)) + (2 * gcd(2, 4)) + (3 * gcd(3, 6)) = 1 + 4 + 9
	= 14
</pre>

Constraints:

    - <code>1 &lt;= n &lt;= 7</code>
    - <code>nums.length == 2 * n</code>
    - <code>1 &lt;= nums[i] &lt;= 10<sup>6</sup></code>
